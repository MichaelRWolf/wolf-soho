# Day 113 Plan - Michael's Atlassian Colleagues

## Message via LinkedIn

Hi — hope you’re doing well. I’ve been technomadic lately, currently in Eastern time zone.

I’d enjoy catching up sometime, but first a quick favor given your Atlassian background.

A colleague lost a long-standing Confluence site under circumstances that don’t line up with direct written advice about keeping accounts active.

Despite logging in and actively using the site, it became inaccessible around Day 44 and was permanently deleted shortly thereafter. A month of back-and-forth with multiple Atlassian teams hasn’t clarified why.

This wasn’t an ephemeral space. It held years of active professional material — seminars, workshops, and teaching content that’s been reused over time and is now being repurposed into a book.

A LinkedIn post earlier this month reached ~70,000 impressions and surfaced a lot of discussion, but nothing that explained why a site with a 5+ year history appears to have followed a much shorter lifecycle than the advertised 120-day window.

At this point the focus is on two practical goals:

* **Data recovery** — if any path still exists, even given conflicting information about deletion schedules
* **Data loss prevention** — so other long-standing users don’t encounter the same surprise

My working hypothesis is that an internal admin or config change routed the account into a different lifecycle path than its original provisioning, and those internal ops processes are not visible to customer-facing teams.  But that's only a hypothesis.

If you know of anyone who understands how long-term Confluence sites could be routed into a short deletion window, I’d appreciate a pointer.

Thanks,
Michael

# Confluence Cloud Free Site Deletion: What Happened and What Support Confirmed

This document records a real experience involving the suspension and permanent deletion of a long-standing Confluence Cloud Free site in late 2025.

It exists for one reason: to help other users understand data-loss risk earlier than I did, and to document what Atlassian support confirmed after the deletion occurred.

I do not expect this post to make my data be magically recovered.  
My goal is simpler: that fewer people lose theirs.

Everything below is based on written support responses, case records, and observed behavior.

---

## Quick Summary

I was told by customer service that Free plans deactivate after 120 days of inactivity. The following day, I used Confluence. Despite this, 44 days after that confirmed use the site was cancelled, and 58 days after that use the data was permanently deleted due to “inactivity.”

- A long-standing (over 5 year) Confluence Cloud Free site was deactivated and permanently deleted due to inactivity.
- Recent written guidance stated a 120-day inactivity window; support later confirmed the window had been reduced to about 40 days.
- This change was not discoverable in public documentation at the time of deletion. I did receive three emails about deactivation, but did not see them until too late.
- Multiple Atlassian teams were engaged over several weeks with intent to recover data and to investigate what happened.
- All teams stated that no recovery path exists once deletion completes.

This document is shared so others can take preventative action sooner.

---

## Basic timeline of events related to the Confluence deletion

### September 29, 2025 (Day -1)

An Atlassian Customer Advocate, responding to a Billing and Licensing support request, stated in writing:

> “Confluence on a Free plan will be deactivated after 120 days of inactivity. To prevent your site from being deactivated, just log in and view any Confluence page. That will count as activity and reset the 120-day clock.”

### September 30, 2025 (Day 0)

I used my Confluence site extensively. Atlassian later confirmed this activity occurred.

### November 13, 2025 (Day 44)

According to Atlassian, the Confluence subscription was cancelled on this date due to inactivity. I was not aware of this at the time.

### November 27, 2025 (Day 58)

Following a 14-day soft deletion window after cancellation, the Confluence site and its data were permanently deleted.  
This date is derived from the cancellation date plus the 14-day soft deletion period and was not known to me at the time.

### November 30, 2025 (Day 61)

I was informed by a Billing and Licensing team member that the Confluence subscription was cancelled on November 13, 2025 due to inactivity. Login was no longer possible and all site data was inaccessible.

### December 2025

For just over a month I engaged in a sustained effort to understand what had happened and whether recovery was possible. I opened several formal support requests involving multiple Atlassian teams.

### December 30, 2025 (Day 91)

Atlassian Cloud Support informed me that the inactivity period had been updated from 120 days to 40 days and that no options existed to recover the data.

### January 14, 2026 (Day 104)

I was unable to locate any publicly accessible Atlassian documentation describing a 40-day inactivity threshold for Confluence Free plan sites.

### January 28, 2026 (Day 120)

This date marks the originally communicated inactivity window under which the site was expected to remain active based on prior written guidance.

## What this means for other users

- Free does not mean safe  
- Inactivity windows may change  
- Login counts as activity only if backend systems record it that way  
- Email-only warnings are risky  
- Export early, export often

## FAQ 2: Yes, three emails were sent

Yes, Atlassian sent emails warning that my Confluence site would be deactivated. I missed them.

At the same time, I had recently been told in writing that Free sites deactivate after 120 days of inactivity. The emails resembled prior routine notices, and nothing in them made clear that the safety window had collapsed to weeks instead of months.

By the time I realized the change, my data was already gone.

I am not sharing this to deny responsibility, but because many reasonable people could make the same mistake.

---

## FAQ 3: Why I am sharing this

If you use Confluence on a Free plan, export or back up your data.  
Do not assume continuity with past guidance.

Life does not happen in neat, low-noise windows. Systems that silently shorten safety margins are not designed for humans living real lives.
